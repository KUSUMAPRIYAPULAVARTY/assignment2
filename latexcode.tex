 \section{Question No. 15}
Find the real numbers $x,y$ such that \myvec{x\\-y}\myvec{3\\5} is conjugate of \myvec{-6\\-24}
%

\section{Explanation}
The matrix representation of a complex number
\begin{align}
\myvec{a\\b}=\myvec{a&-b\\b&a}\myvec{1\\0}
\end{align}
Conjugate of a complex number
\begin{align}
 \myvec{a\\b} = \myvec{a\\-b}   
\end{align} 
\section{Solution}
The conjugate of \myvec{-6\\-24} is \myvec{-6\\24} 
\begin{align}
 \implies \myvec{x\\-y}\myvec{3\\5} =\myvec{-6\\24}\\  
  \implies \myvec{x\\-y}=\frac{\myvec{-6\\24}}{\myvec{3\\5}}
\end{align}
Using equivalent matrices for complex numbers, we have
\begin{align}
\myvec{x\\-y}=\myvec{-6&-24\\24&-6}{\myvec{3&-5\\5&3}}^{-1}\myvec{1\\0}\\
=\frac{1}{34}\myvec{-6&-24\\24&-6}\myvec{3&5\\-5&3}\myvec{1\\0}\\
=\frac{1}{34}\myvec{102&-102\\102&102}\myvec{1\\0}\\
=\myvec{3&-3\\3&3}\myvec{1\\0}\\
\implies\myvec{x\\-y}=\myvec{3\\3}\\
\text{Therefore,}
x=3,\\ y=-3    
\end{align}
